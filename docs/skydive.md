

To flight a drone we have two main components: a drone and a radio controller.

A drone runs for a limited time on batteries and its operation presents some risk of damage to parts.
A drone simulator, on the other hand, helps learning to pilot a drone, without the risk of damage and no limit imposed by batteries.
We are starting cheaper and safer using a simulator instead of an actual drone.
We chose FPV.SkyDive [ˆ1], a free drone simulator available at Steam. 

Later we may buy or build a real drone, but at this point, a simulator has more benefits for a novice pilot.

A radio controller also runs on batteries and is operation is more complex than a game pad or mouse.
We could use a simulator with a gamepad, mouse, and keyboard, but that would not provide an experience useful to pilot a real drone later.
We chose to buy a radio controller, in a way that we could have the proper experience and the option to match a real drone with this real controller.

We chose RadioMaster Pocket [^2], a simplified radio controller that is affordable and a common entry point for novice pilots.
We chose to buy it from a online seller [ˆ3].

So the decisions were made from the perspective of someone with no flight experience and no previous equipment.
We decide to buy a radio controller that would give more information and experience into understanding drone piloting.
We have decided to postpone the buy or build decision about a real drone.
